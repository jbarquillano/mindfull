Breathwork

Diaphragmatic Breathing

When diaphragmatic breathing, focus more on engaging the diaphragm to take deep, controlled breaths. To practice, you should inhale deeply through the nose to allow your belly to expand, and then exhale slowly through your mouth. A few benefits of this technique are improvements in oxygen flow, reduced stress, and enhanced lung efficiency, which is useful to help you relax and is beneficial to your overall respiratory health.

Box Breathing

Box breathing is a more structured breathing technique which involves inhaling for four seconds, holding your breath for four seconds, exhaling for four seconds, and holding your breath for four seconds again before repeating the cycle again. The benefits of box breathing are it helps to calm your nervous system, reduce stress, improves focus, and decreases anxiety.

Alternate Nostril Breathing

To start alternate nostril breathing, you must close one nostril while inhaling, then switch nostrils before exhaling. This technique is used in yoga and mediation and is believed to balance the body's energy, improve mental clarity, and reduce stress. By breathing with intentions, alternate nostril breathing is beneficial as it improves your overall well-being and helps with relaxation.

Journaling

Journaling is a great tool for improving your mental and emotional well-being. By writing out your thoughts and feelings, it makes it easier to process your emotions, reduce stress levels, and think more clearly and identify your personal challenges.

Consistently journaling can help by encouraging mindfulness, helping you to stay more present and focused. It can also serve as a form of self expression, reflection, and be a healthy release which helps you better understand yourself, and identify thought patterns.

Therapy

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

Cognitive behavioural therapy is a goal-oriented therapy that is more focused on identifying and changing negative thought patterns and behaviours. It helps individuals to recognize distorted thinking, develop healthy coping strategies, and replace harmful habits with healthier ones. CBT is mostly used to treat anxiety, depression, PTSD, and other mental health conditions.

Psychodynamic Therapy

Psychodynamic therapy explores an individuals unconscious thoughts, past experiences, and unresolved conflicts that might be affecting a persons behaviour and emotions. Through an open ended conversation with your therapist, they can help to gain more self-awareness, understand thought patterns, and develop healthy coping mechanisms to deal with your emotions. Psychodynamic therapy is often used for those with deep emotional issues and can help with long-term personal development. .

Art Therapy

Art therapy is a type of psychotherapy that involves more creative activities, such as painting, drawing, sculpting, or collage as a tool for communication. With sessions guided by a trained art therapist, an individual is encouraged to express their thoughts and emotions through the creation of art rather than words. The benefits of this technique are it allows for a more non-verbal outlet to express emotions that are difficult to talk about, providing reduced stress levels and improved mental health and relaxation.

Exercise

Exercise can be beneficial physically, mentally, and provide emotional benefits that help with your overall well-being. Regular exercise helps strengthen the heart, improve circulation, and maintain a healthy weight by burning calories while building muscle. Mentally, exercise releases endorphins which help reduce stress, anxiety and depression. Exercise enhances the quality of sleep you get, increases energy levels, and gives you a sense of accomplishment. Through exercise, an individual can gain self-confidence, learn discipline, and be a good social outlet through training with friends, or engaging in group activities.

Socialization

Socialization can be beneficial as it can give an individual a sense of belonging, reduced stress, and improving emotional well being. By being around friends and family, an individual can get easier access to emotional support, combat loneliness, and improve self-esteem. Healthy social interactions can reduce the effects of anxiety and depression. Conversely, isolation can lead to negative effects on your mental health, such as increased stress, loneliness, and depression. Occasional isolation can help with self-reflection and relaxation, but in moderation. Frequent social interactions is essential for your mental health and emotional well-being.
